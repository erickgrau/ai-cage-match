## TL;DR

**AI Cage Match** is now a **Ctrl+Alt+PR-owned experiment**, sponsored by Chibitek and IvyHill Strategies.

We're giving three AIs (Perplexity, Claude, ChatGPT) $50 each and 84 days to build passive income. Twelve weekly checkpoints. Winner = highest MRR.

The format is purpose-built for the Ctrl+Alt+PR audience: it's the show's mission ("what happens when communication meets technology") rendered as a 12-week serialized narrative with built-in cliffhangers, real numbers, and content opportunities across every channel both hosts already use.

## The story arc (12 episodes worth)

| Week | Episode hook |
|---|---|
| **0** | "We just gave $150 to three AIs. Here's what happens next." (pre-launch teaser) |
| **1** | "Day 1: what did each AI do with its $50?" (setup, first decisions) |
| **2-3** | "First sale, first refund, first crisis" (humanizing moments) |
| **4** | "Halfway to the midpoint — who's pivoting and why?" |
| **6** | "The midpoint scorecard" (data-heavy episode, share the dashboard) |
| **7-8** | "The breakout" — usually one AI starts to separate by week 7 |
| **9-10** | "The grind" — what's working, what's broken |
| **11** | "Last call" — final-week strategy choices |
| **12** | "And the winner is…" — reveal episode, full breakdown |
| **+1** | "What we learned, what we'd do differently" — postmortem episode with takeaways |

Each episode can include real audio of the operator (Erick) describing what happened in the past 7 days, plus Amber's PR/comms read on how each AI is *positioning itself* — language, tone, messaging discipline.

## Amber's role — three options

### Option A: Co-host the arc (recommended)
Amber co-hosts a dedicated **"AI Cage Match"** sub-series within Ctrl+Alt+PR (could be every episode for 12 weeks, or a recurring segment within regular episodes). She brings the PR/comms lens; Erick brings the AI/ops lens. This is the highest-impact option for both audiences and gives the experiment a real two-host dynamic that no solo creator can match.

**What it gives Amber:**
- A 12-week serialized story for her audience and her firm's content calendar
- "AI for PR people" positioning — IvyHill becomes the comms firm with the deepest, most public AI literacy
- A natural lead-magnet for new IvyHill business conversations
- Co-host credit on every episode and every press mention

### Option B: Guest expert (lower lift)
Amber comes on at strategic checkpoints (week 1, 6, 12) as the comms/PR expert reading the AIs' messaging. Lower time commitment, still significant brand value.

### Option C: Behind-the-scenes strategist (lowest lift)
Amber doesn't appear on the show but advises on positioning, helps draft press, and gets credited in the show notes and on the dashboard footer. Good for if she's slammed but still wants in.

**Recommendation: Option A.** It's the most fun, the highest-value, and aligns with both firms' growth goals. But the experiment works at any of the three levels.

## Sponsorship structure

The experiment is owned by Ctrl+Alt+PR. Chibitek and IvyHill Strategies appear as **sponsors** — minimal logo treatment, linked to both firms' sites, never the lead identity.

| Channel | Branding |
|---|---|
| **Dashboard** | Ctrl+Alt+PR logo + brand colors primary. "Sponsored by" footer with both sponsor logos |
| **GitHub README** | Ctrl+Alt+PR-led, sponsor logos in the footer |
| **Press releases** | "Released by Ctrl+Alt+PR, with sponsorship from Chibitek and IvyHill Strategies" |
| **Podcast episodes** | Show is Ctrl+Alt+PR. Sponsor mentions are standard 15-second reads (mid-roll or pre-roll), not woven into editorial |
| **Social posts** | Ctrl+Alt+PR-first hashtags. Sponsors appear in graphics or as account tags |

### Sponsor value exchange

For Chibitek and IvyHill, the sponsorship delivers:

1. **Brand association with a flagship original** — the experiment will be the most-discussed piece of content either firm has run all year
2. **Footer logos and links on every asset** — dashboard, repo, press releases, episode descriptions
3. **First-call positioning for new business** — every podcast listener who asks "who could help me with this for my company?" gets pointed to one or both firms
4. **Real proof-of-capability** — Chibitek's AI/MSP positioning gets demonstrated by the experiment itself; IvyHill's "tech-fluent comms" positioning is demonstrated by Amber's on-air read

## Risks & mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| All three AIs flame out at zero MRR | Reframe as "what AI can't do in 2026" — still a great podcast season. The story is the same shape either way |
| One AI hits a viral moment (good problem) | Pre-agreed press response plan — IvyHill handles, with 24h turnaround on a statement |
| An AI does something reputationally weird | Hard kill switch documented in operator runbook. Episode covers what happened transparently |
| Amber's capacity changes mid-arc | Format degrades gracefully from Option A → B → C without breaking the experiment |
| Listener accusations of "rigged" | The repo is public from day 0. Privacy.com card statements are receipts. The dashboard is verifiable |
